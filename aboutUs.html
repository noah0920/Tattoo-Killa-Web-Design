Jolyne Johnson

Gothic Artist

The Gothic style represents the Gothic subculture and is a way of expressing feelings and emotions through mystical symbols and figures. In gothic tattoos there is a great study of light and shadow.

Bruce Smith

Realism Artist

The goal is to express the reality of the characters/scenes/situations in the skin, paying special attention to small details that may go unnoticed to the eyes of others.

Sophie Williams

Japanese Style Artist

Traditional Japanese style reflects images of masks, samurai, geishas, red fish, devils, dragons... With profusion of colors, a lot of realism and with detailed and very sophisticated designs.

Connor Murphy

Black Work Artist

The blackwork, as its name suggests, is a "black work", that is, it is a style of tattooing in which only black ink is used. It is characterized by the use of high contrasts, without shadows or gray tones. The lines are very precise and gradients are applied with textures, dots or patterns.
